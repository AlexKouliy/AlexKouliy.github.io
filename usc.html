Overview

Unified Science of Colour (USC) is a groundbreaking infographic book that unites scientific inquiry with artistic exploration. USC reveals that colour is, at its core, simply light—wavelengths interacting with matter—to unravel the complex, multifaceted role colour plays in art, design, and human emotion.

Born from a personal quest to decode the mysteries of colour through the Munsell system and beyond, this project provides active learning exercises, regular recaps, and a comprehensive guide that bridges disciplines. It's your all-in-one codex to mastering colour.

Description

USC explores the external forces that create colour—tracing the journey of photons, the interplay of light and pigment, and the historical evolution of colour theory. Discover how digital screens emulate these natural processes and how traditional art harnesses them through pigment and technique.

The book features an extensive, visually engaging infographic (see below) that breaks down the scientific, cultural, and psychological aspects of colour in a clear, digestible format.

USC is designed to empower artists, designers, and creative thinkers by providing a clear, accessible pathway through the complex science of colour. It connects the dots between the physics of light, the chemistry of pigments, and the psychology of colour perception.

By combining historical insights, modern research, and practical exercises, USC transforms theoretical knowledge into practical design wisdom. Whether you’re a student or a seasoned professional, USC offers the tools to deepen your creative practice.

Rationale

  • Addresses the gap between scientific research and practical artistic application, bridging these worlds through accessible and engaging content that fosters deeper creative exploration.
  • Supports artists and designers by providing them with scientifically grounded insights to strategically and effectively harness colour, enhancing their creative practices.
  • Positions colour not merely as an aesthetic choice but as a multifaceted tool shaped by deep psychological, cultural, and scientific influences.
  • Utilizes carefully selected colour palettes and visual elements rooted in colour psychology, enhancing learning retention and emotional engagement.
  • Employs clear typography, intuitive layouts, and gestalt principles to ensure maximum readability, comprehension, and accessibility for diverse users, including neurodiverse and visually sensitive audiences.
  • Aims to become an essential educational resource for visual creators, students, and educators, continually expanding and evolving alongside new scientific discoveries.
  • Promotes an integrative approach to colour, advocating for informed, intentional design choices and deeper appreciation of colour’s role in human experience, culture, and communication.
